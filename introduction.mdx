## The problem

People discover products everywhere — Instagram, TikTok, screenshots from a friend, a photo on a brand's site. By the time they want to buy it, the source is buried, the price has changed, or they cannot remember where they saw it.

The product information exists in the image. Extracting it manually is tedious. Searching for it again is unreliable.

## What Entropi does

Entropi is an HTTP API that takes a product screenshot and returns structured product data: name, price, currency, merchant, product URL, and a clean cropped image.

You upload an image. You get back a `product_id` in under 500&nbsp;ms. The full pipeline (OCR, crop, web enrichment) runs in the background. You poll until the product is `ready`.

## Mission

Save anything you see. Find it instantly. Buy it when you want to.

We exist to remove the friction between visual discovery and ownership. Every product anyone has ever shown you should be one tap away from your shortlist.

## Who this is for

This documentation is written for the people who build, run, and integrate against Entropi:

- **Engineers** — backend, mobile, and frontend developers integrating the API or contributing to it.
- **Operators** — anyone deploying or self-hosting Entropi.
- **Partners** — external teams calling the public API.

It is not a marketing site. It assumes you know how to read a `curl` command, run a Python script, and interpret an HTTP status code.

## What you can do in five minutes

By the end of the [Quickstart](/getting-started/quickstart) you will have:

1. Registered a user and obtained a JWT.
2. Created a list.
3. Uploaded a product screenshot.
4. Polled the pipeline until it returned a fully enriched product.

If something does not work in those five minutes, that is a documentation bug. Open an issue.
